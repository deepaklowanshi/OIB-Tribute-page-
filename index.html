The Life and Legacy of Mahatma Gandhi

Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi, better known as Mahatma Gandhi, was a key figure in India's struggle for independence from British rule. His philosophy of nonviolent resistance has inspired movements for civil rights and freedom across the world.

Born on October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, India, Gandhi dedicated his life to promoting peace, harmony, and justice. Through his teachings of truth, nonviolence, and compassion, he led the Indian National Congress and millions of people in the nonviolent disobedience campaign against British colonial rule.

Gandhi's impact extended beyond India, influencing leaders like Martin Luther King Jr. and Nelson Mandela. He was a symbol of simplicity, humility, and unity, and his legacy continues to inspire generations.
